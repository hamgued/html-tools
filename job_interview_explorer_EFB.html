Skills and competences — Employers look for two types of skills: soft skills and hard skills. You need both to do a job well. Use this page to learn what they mean and how to talk about them in an interview.
🧠 Soft skills 20 skills

Soft skills are personal qualities that help you work well with other people and deal with different situations. You use these skills in almost every job.

🗣️
Communication
You can share ideas clearly, both when speaking and writing. You listen well and make sure others understand you.
"I am a clear communicator — I always check that my message is understood."
🤝
Teamwork
You work well with other people. You share tasks, support your colleagues, and work toward a common goal.
"I enjoy working in teams because different people bring different ideas."
🧩
Problem-solving
You can look at a difficult situation, think of different options, and find a good solution.
"When a problem comes up, I stay calm and focus on finding a practical solution."
🔄
Adaptability
You can change easily when the situation changes. You are comfortable with new tasks, tools, or people.
"I adapt quickly — when our project changed direction, I adjusted my work without difficulty."
⏰
Time management
You can organise your work and finish tasks on time. You know which tasks are most important and do those first.
"I use a weekly plan to make sure I meet all my deadlines."
🌟
Initiative
You start things yourself without waiting to be asked. You see what needs to be done and do it.
"I noticed a gap in our process and proposed a solution before anyone asked me to."
🪞
Self-awareness
You understand your own strengths and weaknesses. You know how your behaviour affects other people.
"I know that I sometimes work too fast, so I now take time to check my work carefully."
🎯
Goal-setting
You can decide what you want to achieve, make a plan, and work towards it step by step.
"I set clear goals for each semester and track my progress regularly."
💬
Active listening
You give your full attention when someone is speaking. You do not interrupt and you ask questions to make sure you understand.
"I always listen carefully before I answer — it helps me give better responses."
🤔
Critical thinking
You think carefully before you decide or act. You look at information from different sides and do not accept everything without questioning.
"Before making a decision, I always consider the possible risks and benefits."
💪
Resilience
You can deal with difficulties and keep going even when things are hard. You learn from failure instead of giving up.
"When I failed my first attempt, I analysed what went wrong and tried again with a better approach."
🌍
Cultural awareness
You understand that people have different backgrounds and ways of working. You respect these differences and work well with people from different cultures.
"Working in international teams has helped me understand and respect different communication styles."
🙌
Empathy
You can understand how other people feel. This helps you communicate better and support your colleagues.
"I try to understand my teammates' situations before giving feedback."
📣
Presentation skills
You can share information clearly in front of a group. You speak in a way that is easy to follow and keep people's attention.
"I have practised presenting by giving short talks in class and at the Business Fair."
🔍
Attention to detail
You check your work carefully and notice small errors. You do not rush and make sure everything is correct before finishing.
"I always proofread my written work twice before submitting it."
🔁
Willingness to learn
You are open to learning new things, even when they are difficult. You see feedback as a chance to improve.
"I asked for feedback after every draft and used it to improve my writing."
⚡
Conflict management
You can deal with disagreements calmly and professionally. You try to find a solution that works for everyone.
"When there was a disagreement in the group, I suggested a meeting so everyone could share their views."
🧭
Decision-making
You can look at a situation, consider your options, and choose the best action. You take responsibility for your choices.
"I had to decide which tasks to prioritise. I chose based on the deadline and the impact."
📋
Organisation
You keep your work and information in order. You plan ahead and manage your tasks so nothing is forgotten.
"I use a shared document to track tasks and deadlines for all my projects."
😊
Positive attitude
You approach challenges with energy and optimism. Even in difficult moments, you focus on what can be done instead of what cannot.
"Even when the project was behind schedule, I encouraged the team to keep going."
🔧 Hard skills 15 skills

Hard skills are specific abilities you learn through study, training, or practice. They are often connected to a particular job or subject area.

🌐
Business English
You can communicate in English in a professional context — writing emails, reports, and taking part in meetings or interviews.
"I have studied Business English and can write professional reports and emails."
📊
Data analysis
You can collect and study information (numbers, results, trends) and explain what it means.
"I used data from a survey to identify the most common customer problems."
💻
Microsoft Office / Google Workspace
You can use tools like Word, Excel, PowerPoint, or Google Docs, Sheets, and Slides to create documents, spreadsheets, and presentations.
"I use Excel to track budgets and create clear tables and charts."
📝
Report writing
You can plan, structure, and write a professional report that is clear, accurate, and well-organised.
"I wrote a business plan report during my studies, which included research and structured sections."
💰
Basic financial literacy
You understand basic financial concepts such as budgets, costs, revenue, and profit.
"I understand how to read a simple budget and identify where costs can be reduced."
📣
Marketing basics
You understand how businesses attract customers, including ideas like target audience, channels, and value proposition.
"I studied the basics of marketing and created a customer segment table for my business plan."
🔎
Research skills
You can find, read, and evaluate sources of information. You know which sources are reliable and which are not.
"I found and evaluated sources for my report using online databases and websites."
🗂️
Project management basics
You understand how to plan a project, divide tasks, set deadlines, and track progress.
"I managed a group project by creating a timeline and making sure everyone knew their role."
🤖
AI tool use
You can use AI tools to help with tasks like checking writing, getting feedback, or researching a topic. You understand the limits of AI and use it responsibly.
"I used an AI tool to get feedback on my draft report and then rewrote it in my own words."
📞
Customer communication
You can communicate clearly and professionally with customers or clients — in writing, by phone, or in person.
"I handled customer questions in my part-time job, which improved my professional communication."
🌍
Foreign language skills
You can communicate in one or more languages other than your first language. This is especially useful in international business.
"I speak Finnish, English, and basic Spanish, which has been useful in customer-facing roles."
📈
Sales fundamentals
You understand the basics of how selling works — identifying customer needs, presenting a product, and handling questions or objections.
"I learned about the sales process through my business plan project and the Business Fair."
🖥️
Social media literacy
You understand how social media platforms work and how businesses use them to communicate with customers.
"I have experience managing social media content and know how to write posts that engage an audience."
📐
Process thinking
You can look at how something is done and identify clear steps or improvements. You think logically about how systems and workflows operate.
"I mapped out our team's workflow and suggested a simpler process that saved time."
🎨
Visual presentation
You can create clear, professional-looking presentations or documents using tools like PowerPoint or Canva.
"I designed the slides for my Business Fair presentation to make the information easy to follow."
🔐
Digital literacy
You are comfortable using digital tools and platforms. You can learn new software quickly and use technology to work more efficiently.
"I picked up the project management tool quickly and helped my team use it effectively."
📬
Professional email writing
You can write clear, polite, and well-structured emails in a professional style.
"I have practised writing professional emails in English, including follow-up and thank-you messages."
🏢
Business model understanding
You understand how businesses create and deliver value — including concepts like value proposition, customer segments, and revenue models.
"I studied the Business Model Canvas and applied it to my own imaginary business idea."
🧾
Academic and professional writing
You can write in a formal, clear, and structured way — using correct grammar, citations, and paragraph organisation.
"I completed a structured academic report and learned how to write definitions, arguments, and reflections."
🤝
Networking
You can build and maintain professional relationships with people in your field. You know how to introduce yourself and have professional conversations.
"I practised professional networking at the Business Fair by presenting my idea to different people."
