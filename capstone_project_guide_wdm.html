What is this assignment?
🎯 Objective
▾

You create a website for an imaginary organisation or company that offers something for university students. You produce content in English, promote your website, and interact with and give feedback to your peers.

The content can be about anything — as long as it targets students and is written in English. Be creative!

Step 1: Create Customer Personas

3 personas Target: university students

Develop three audience/customer personas to clearly identify the main target groups for your website. Your organisation must offer something for university students.

A persona is a detailed profile of a fictional user — including name, age, background, goals, and challenges. Personas help you write content that speaks to your audience.
📚 What to include in a persona
▾
  • Name and photo (can be fictional/AI-generated)
  • Age, study field, and year of study
  • Goals and motivations
  • Pain points and challenges
  • How your product/service helps them

Step 2: Set Up a Website

Google Sites 4 pages total

Set up a Google Sites website where you will publish all four pages of content. Sign in at sites.google.com using your LAB/LUT credentials.

Although the main focus is on writing, do your best to make the site visually engaging. Google Sites has limitations — that is understood.

Step 3: Create an About Us Page

Page 1 ~400 words Narrative style + Timeline

Write a short origin story of your organisation. Make it engaging — include at least one challenge or change the organisation has overcome.

✍️ Writing guidance
▾
  • Aim for mostly narrative style — tell a story
  • ~400 words (equivalent to ~1 page in Word with standard formatting)
  • At least one challenge/obstacle the organisation overcame
  • Feel free to be creative — the organisation is imaginary!
📅 Timeline requirement
▾

Include a timeline visualising 6–8 key dates in your organisation's history. This can be created using Google Sites, a Canva template, or any other visual tool.

Search "company history timeline" on Google Images for inspiration.

📌 Before moving on: Publish the page, add a brief summary, and include the notice: "This website is a learning project created in the Writing for Digital Media course at LAB University of Applied Sciences/LUT University"

Step 4: Start a Discussion Thread (Message 1)

Promotional writing Message 1 of 4

Start a discussion thread in Moodle: Capstone Project: Promote Your Website. Post Message 1 — announcing that your company's new website is live.

Think of this like a social media post. It should be short, punchy, and drive readers to your site. Although not Twitter/X, apply similar principles: concise, engaging, action-oriented.
💬 Tips for promotional messages
▾
  • Keep it short and direct
  • Highlight the value for your audience (university students)
  • Include a call to action (e.g. "Visit us at…")
  • Use an engaging hook — a question, a bold claim, or a surprising fact

You will post 4 messages in total — one after completing each website page.

Step 5: Create a Products/Services Page

Page 2 ~400 words Expository + persuasive + descriptive + Images

Write a short introduction to your product or service. Describe its key characteristics (size, price, colour, qualities, features…). Include at least one claim supported by evidence (the evidence can be made up).

✍️ Writing guidance
▾
  • Use expository, descriptive, and persuasive writing styles
  • Make at least one claim — e.g. "Product X is 50% more student-friendly than any similar product"
  • Support the claim with some evidence (real or imagined)
  • Include images to visualise the product or service
After publishing this page, post Message 2 in your discussion thread — announcing the update and promoting the product/service.
📌 Before moving on: Publish the page, add a brief summary, and include the notice: "This website is a learning project created in the Writing for Digital Media course at LAB University of Applied Sciences/LUT University"

Step 6: Create a How-to-Use Page

Page 3 ~400 words + 3–4 min video YouTube

Write instructions on how to use or order your product/service, and record a short video explaining the instructions in English.

✍️ Written instructions
▾
  • ~400 words of step-by-step instructions
  • Expository style (primarily), with persuasive elements
  • Include images to illustrate the steps
🎬 Video instructions
▾
  • 3–4 minutes long
  • Can be a screen recording of a PowerPoint presentation — your face does not need to appear
  • Upload to YouTube and embed the video on Page 3 of your website
  • Edit the YouTube auto-captions for accuracy (YouTube generates them automatically)
Do not skip caption editing — it is part of the requirement.
After publishing, post Message 3 in your discussion thread.
📌 Before moving on: Publish the page, add a brief summary, and include the notice: "This website is a learning project created in the Writing for Digital Media course at LAB University of Applied Sciences/LUT University"

Step 7: Collect Data and Discuss Your Findings

Page 4 Google Forms survey Min. 15 participants ~400 words (data write-up)

Design a short Google Forms survey (3–5 questions) to collect data from university students on how they see your organisation and its products/services. Then write a short text discussing what the data shows.

📊 Survey design
▾
  • 3–5 questions
  • Briefly introduce your organisation and product/service in the form intro
  • Aim for at least 15 participants
  • Share via friends, classmates, and the course's shared link page
📝 Data commentary
▾
  • ~400 words — describe what the data shows and what it means
  • Include graphs based on your collected data
After publishing, post Message 4 in your discussion thread.

🗺️ PAIRR Step 1 — Understand AI & Your Work
Before you give or receive feedback · See the Your Work tab
▾

Before entering the feedback loop, make sure you understand where your work sits in relation to AI use. Go to the Your Work tab and work through the Writing Spectrum and scenario activity.

This step is not assessed directly, but it helps you reflect on your process and prepare your AI transparency statement.
💬 PAIRR Step 2 — Peer Review
Visit at least 3 peers' websites · Use the Feedback Sandwich
▾

Visit at least 3 other students' websites. Start with someone who has not received feedback yet. Apply the Feedback Sandwich:

  1. Positive Feedback — what has your peer done well?
  2. Constructive Criticism — specific, useful suggestions for improvement
  3. Final Positive Note — end with encouragement

Focus on:

  • All required content present?
  • User-friendly writing: headings, lists, bold keywords, short paragraphs, white space
  • Proofread — obvious errors?
  • Visual neatness and professionalism
Tips for giving good peer feedback:
  • Be specific — "the About Us page reads like a list" is more useful than "it is not engaging."
  • Comment on content and structure, not only language.
  • Start with what works well before suggesting improvements.
🤖 PAIRR Step 3 — AI Feedback
Get AI feedback on all four pages of your website
▾

Ask an AI tool for feedback on all four pages of your website. Use your @lab or @lut account for GDPR-compliant access where possible.

Your instructor may provide a ready-made feedback chatbot for this step. If so, use that first — it is set up with the assignment goals in mind.
What to do:
  1. Paste the text of each page into the AI tool (one at a time or all together, depending on the tool).
  2. Use a specific feedback prompt — do not ask it to rewrite your text.
  3. Note which suggestions feel useful and which do not.
Example prompt:
You are reviewing a page from a student website created for a university writing course. The student's organisation offers a product or service aimed at university students.

Please give feedback under these four headings:

1. Content — Is the content complete and relevant? Does it include what the page requires (e.g. a supported claim on the Products page, a clear origin story on About Us)?
2. Writing style — Is the writing style appropriate for this page (narrative / expository / persuasive / descriptive)? Is the tone engaging for a student audience?
3. Web writing — Is the text easy to scan? Consider: use of headings, short paragraphs, bullet points, bold keywords, and white space.
4. Language — Are there any grammar, punctuation, or spelling issues? Is the language clear and in plain English?

Do not rewrite the text. Keep each section brief (3–5 bullet points). End with one overall strength and one priority improvement.

Here is the page text: [paste your text here]

The proofreading prompt in the Your Work tab is also useful here for language-specific feedback.

🔍 PAIRR Step 4 — Compare & Reflect
A reflection in your PDF submission (~400 words)
▾

In your PDF submission, include a reflection comparing the peer feedback and the AI feedback you received — approximately 400 words. Write in continuous prose, not bullet points. Use these questions to guide you:

  • What did your peer notice that the AI did not — or vice versa?
  • Which type of feedback was more useful for content and ideas?
  • Which was more useful for language and clarity?
  • Did the two ever contradict each other? How did you decide what to do?
  • What does this tell you about the strengths and limits of AI feedback compared to human feedback?
  • Which suggestions did you choose to act on, and why? Which did you decide to ignore or change? What was your reason?
  • Looking back, how would you use peer and AI feedback in future writing tasks?
Remember: You are in charge. You decide which suggestions to use and which to leave out. There is no obligation to accept every piece of feedback — from your peer or from the AI. What matters is that you can explain your choices.

Table 1 — Peer feedback vs. AI feedback: a quick reference

DimensionPeer feedbackAI feedback
Content & voiceOften stronger — peers react as real readers and understand your contextTends to be generic — may miss what makes your organisation unique
Language & clarityDepends on your peer's language levelUsually consistent and detailed
AccuracyLess likely to invent informationCan sound confident while being wrong — always verify
Include this reflection alongside your AI transparency statement in the PDF.
