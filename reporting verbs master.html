The Strength Spectrum

Reporting verbs differ in terms of their strength. For example, 'to suggest' is much weaker and more tentative than 'to argue'.

Essential Grammar Rules

1. The Third-Person 'S'

When the subject is singular (e.g., Smith, the author), add an 's'.

✅ Smith argues...
❌ Smith argue...

2. Present Tense Choice

Usually, we use the Present Simple to bring past research into the current conversation.

✅ "Jones suggests that..."
