Academic writing at LAB must be clear, factual, and consistent. These rule cards cover the core conventions you need to apply throughout your thesis.

Language Quality

The language must be easy to read, clear, concrete, and consistent. Avoid spoken-language expressions. Use professional vocabulary of your field. Prefer direct constructions — e.g., is evaluated over evaluation is carried out. Avoid vague phrases like within the framework of.

✗ Avoid

An evaluation of the results was carried out by the research team within the framework of the project.

✓ Prefer

The research team evaluated the results.

Paragraph & Chapter Rules

Text must progress consistently sentence-to-sentence and paragraph-to-paragraph. Each chapter must contain at least two paragraphs, and each paragraph must contain at least two sentences.

✗ Avoid

3 Customer Satisfaction
Customer satisfaction is important.

One paragraph, one sentence — invalid.

✓ Prefer

3 Customer Satisfaction
Customer satisfaction measures how well a company meets expectations. It is a key indicator of long-term business success.

Several factors influence satisfaction, including product quality and service speed. These are examined in the following sections.

Paraphrasing vs. Quoting

Paraphrasing shows you understood the source. Short direct quotations inside a sentence are typed in italics. Long direct quotations use the LAB Citation style, separated by blank lines before and after. Translated quotations are not italicised — reference as (Author Year, author's translation).

✗ Avoid — copy-pasting

Leadership is the process of influencing others to understand and agree about what needs to be done and how to do it. (Yukl 2013, 7.)

Overuse of direct quotation adds no analysis.

✓ Prefer — paraphrase

According to Yukl (2013, 7), leadership involves guiding others toward a shared understanding of goals and the methods required to achieve them.

Short in-sentence quotation (italics)

Yukl (2013, 7) defines leadership as the process of influencing others to understand and agree.

Translated quotation (no italics)

Leadership is the process of influencing others toward shared goals. (Yukl 2013, 7, author's translation.)

Lists

Lists must have an introductory sentence or phrase. No comma after bullets. Bullets not full sentences → start lowercase, full stop after last bullet only. Bullets that are full sentences → capital letter + full stop each. An entire chapter cannot consist of lists only.

✗ Avoid

The system has several features:
• Fast processing,
• Secure login,
• Easy export.

Commas after bullets — incorrect.

✓ Prefer

The system has several features:
• fast processing
• secure login
• easy export.

No commas. Lowercase start. Full stop after last bullet only.

✓ Full-sentence bullets

The following stages take place in forming groups:
• First, members who do not know each other are overly courteous.
• Next, conflicts arise and members begin to test each other.
• In the last stage, the situation stabilises and the group is ready to work productively.

Illustrations — Images, Figures, Tables

All illustrations are left-aligned and numbered consecutively. Figure/image captions go below; table captions go above. Style: LAB Figure Title. Captions are not complete sentences. Reference every illustration by number in the text before it appears. Must fit on one page with caption — otherwise place in appendices.

Figure caption — below
[ chart / diagram ]

Figure 1. Sales volume by quarter (Smith 2022)

Caption is below the figure. Not a complete sentence.

Table caption — above

Table 1. Annual revenue figures (Jones 2021)

[ table data ]

Caption is above the table.

In-text reference before the illustration

Customer satisfaction scores varied significantly across regions, as shown in Figure 1.

Always refer to the illustration by number in the text before it appears.

Master the rules for citing sources within your thesis text. Each rule card covers a specific citation pattern used in LAB theses.

Parenthetical — Single Sentence ?The reference applies to one sentence only. The full stop ends the sentence after the closing bracket.

Full stop goes outside the closing bracket.

Good management helps employees develop competence (Ekholm & Salmenkangas 2015, 35–36).
Parenthetical — Multiple Sentences / Paragraph ?When the reference covers more than one sentence, the full stop moves inside the bracket to signal the wider scope of the citation.

Full stop goes inside the brackets: (Tuomi-Sorjonen 2013, 53.)

Example — two sentences paraphrased from one source

Value-added tax included in representation costs is not deductible. Representation expenses are costs that aim to create new business relationships, maintain existing ones, or otherwise promote business. (Tuomi-Sorjonen 2013, 53.)

The reference covers both sentences, so the full stop goes inside the closing bracket.

Narrative Reference ?The author's name is woven into the sentence as its subject. The year and page go in brackets directly after the name — not at the end of the sentence.

Author name in the sentence; year and page in brackets immediately after.

Myers and Kassing (2009, 72) claim that…
According to Myers and Kassing (2009, 72), …
Singh et al. (2020) emphasize that…
Multiple Sources in One Reference ?Listing sources chronologically shows the development of research over time. Separate each source with a semicolon inside the same pair of brackets.

List in chronological order, separated by semicolons.

(Siukosaari 2015, 205; Ojanen 2016, 18; Kuutti 2017, 102.)
Two Authors ?Use an ampersand (&) between two authors in both in-text references and in the references list. Never use "and".

Use an ampersand: (Chang & Nguyen 2011, 13.)

Three or More Authors ?Only the first-listed author is named in the in-text reference. "et al." is Latin for "and others". Note: et al. is NOT permitted in the references list — all authors must be named there.

First author only + et al.: (Jones et al. 2009, 43–45.)

Secondary Sources ?A secondary source is one you read that quotes or cites the original (primary) source. Always try to find the primary source first. Only the secondary source appears in the references list.

Use only when primary source is unavailable.
(Robson 1995, according to Hirsjärvi et al. 2009, 134.)
Only the secondary source goes in the references list.

Acts and Decrees ?Include the official name of the act, its code number, the year, and the relevant section. No page numbers are used for legislative sources.

(Biobank Act 688/2012, Section 2.)

No Author Found ?If no individual author is named, use the title of the publication. For web pages without an author, use the name of the organisation responsible for the site.

Use publication title: (Travel Guide to the Galaxy 2012, 14.)
Web page — use publishing body + year: (Posti Group Oyj 2016)

Same Author, Same Year ?Add lowercase letters (a, b, c…) immediately after the year whenever the same author has multiple works published in the same year. Use the same letters in both the in-text reference and the references list.

Add lowercase letters: (Nguyen 2016a, 23; Nguyen 2016b, 35.)

How to format reference list entries for each source type used in LAB theses.

General Rules ?The references list is ordered A–Z by the first author's surname. Every source cited in the text must appear, and no source may appear unless it was actually cited.

Alphabetical order A–Z. All sources used must appear; no unused sources. Authors listed as Surname, I. All authors named — et al. is prohibited in the references list.

Printed Book ?Include edition information if it is not the first edition. If the place of publication or publisher is missing from the source, simply omit that element.

Surname, I. Year. Title. Place: Publisher.

Brynjolfsson, E. & McAfee, A. 2014. The Second Machine Age. New York: W. W. Norton.
Journal / Magazine Article ?For academic journals, include the volume and issue number (e.g. Vol. 13 (8)). For newspaper articles, include the full date and page number instead.

Surname, I. Year. Article title. Journal Name, pages.

Lepore, J. 2020. All the King's Data. The New Yorker, August 3 & 10/2020, 18–24.
Digital Source ?Always include the retrieval date — web content can change or disappear. If access requires a login or payment, write "Limited availability at" instead of "Available at". No full stop after the URL.

Surname, I. Year. Title. Publisher. Retrieved on [date]. Available at [URL]
No full stop after the web address. Always include retrieval date.

Web Page — No Author ?When no year of publication is shown on the page, omit the year entirely — do not write "n.d." or "no date". The organisation name or page title acts as the author.

Use the organisation name or page title.

Finnish Nurses Association. Professional Ethics and Collegiality. Retrieved on 12 August 2020. Available at https://…
Act / Decree ?The minimal entry is just the name and code. You may optionally add the source database and retrieval information for reader convenience.

Name of Act Code/Year.

Biobank Act 688/2012.
Interview (Background Research) ?Only background research interviews are listed here. Interviews conducted to gather empirical research data are kept anonymous and are never included in the references list.
Green, M. 2022. Managing Director. DuraPipes Ltd. Interview on 5 February 2022.

Empirical data interviews are NOT listed — they are anonymous.

Thesis ?Specify the degree level clearly. Theses published on Theseus can be referenced with the URN permalink, which is permanent and preferred over a direct URL.
Harjunpää, A. 2022. [Title]. LAB University of Applied Sciences. Thesis (Master's degree). Retrieved on 2 May 2022. Available at https://…
Images / Figures / Tables ?Sources for all illustrations go in the same references list as all other sources — there is no separate list for images. The format follows the same rules as for the source type (book, web page, etc.).

Sources for illustrations go in the same references list — no separate list.

Nagy, R. R. 2015. Datamancer's Steampunk Laptop. Retrieved on 10 February 2020. Available at http://…
Secondary Source in the Ref List ?You only list the source you actually read (the secondary source). The original author (primary source) is mentioned in the in-text reference but does not appear in the references list.

Enter only the secondary source. If you cited (Robson 1995, according to Hirsjärvi et al. 2009, 134), only Hirsjärvi et al. appears.

Article in an Edited Compilation

Format: Author. Year. Article title. In Editor (Eds.). Book title. Place: Publisher, pages.

Note the "In" before the editor's name. The book title is in italics. All authors named — "et al." is never used here.

Biobank Act 688/2012.

Act / Decree

Format: Name of Act Code/Year.

The minimal entry is the official name and code number. Ends with a full stop. No author, no publisher.

Brynjolfsson, E. & McAfee, A. 2014. The Second Machine Age: Work, Progress, and Prosperity in a Time of Brilliant Technologies. First Edition. New York: W. W. Norton.

Printed Book — Two Authors

Format: Surname, I. & Surname, I. Year. Title. Edition (if not first). Place: Publisher.

Two authors joined with & (ampersand), never "and". Edition included as it is stated in the source.

Finnish Nurses Association. Professional Ethics and Collegiality. Retrieved on 12 August 2020. Available at https://sairaanhoitajat.fi/en/profession-and-skills/professional-ethics-and-collegiality/

Web Page — No Author, No Year

Format: Organisation Name. Page Title. Retrieved on [date]. Available at [URL]

No year found → year is simply omitted; do not write "n.d.". No full stop after the URL.

Green, M. 2022. Managing Director. DuraPipes Ltd. Interview on 5 February 2022.

Interview — Background Research

Format: Surname, I. Year. Title/Position. Organisation. Interview on [date].

Only background research interviews appear here. Empirical data interviews are anonymous and never listed.

Harjunpää, A. 2022. Yksintyöskentelyn tiedonhallinnan ja tietojohtamisen kehittäminen osaksi organisaation toimintastrategiaa. LAB University of Applied Sciences. Thesis (Master's degree). Retrieved on 2 May 2022. Available at https://urn.fi/URN:NBN:fi:amk-202204195388

Thesis (Online)

Format: Surname, I. Year. Title. University. Thesis (degree level). Retrieved on [date]. Available at [URL]

State the degree level clearly. The URN permalink is preferred over a direct URL as it is permanent. No full stop after the link.

Lepore, J. 2020. All the King's Data: Computer forecasting in the 1960 Presidential election. The New Yorker, August 3 & 10/2020, 18–24.

Magazine / Newspaper Article

Format: Surname, I. Year. Article title. Journal/Magazine, issue/volume/date, pages.

For academic journals, use volume and issue number. For magazines, use the full issue date. Always include the page range.

Loxton, M., Truskett, R., Scarf, B., Sindone, L., Baldry, G. & Zhao, Y. 2020. Consumer Behaviour during Crises: Preliminary Research on How Coronavirus Has Manifested Consumer Panic Buying, Herd Mentality, Changing Discretionary Spending and the Role of the Media in Influencing Behaviour. Journal of Risk and Financial Management, Vol. 13 (8), 166. Retrieved on 1 June 2021. Available at DOI 10.3390/jrfm13080166

Online Journal Article — Six Authors

Format: All authors listed with & before the last. Year. Article title. Journal, Vol. X (Y), article/page no. Retrieved on [date]. Available at DOI [link]

All six authors must be named — "et al." is forbidden in the references list. "166" here is an article number, not a page number.

Zhivotov, D. & Tilinin, Y. 2020. Experimental studies of the strength of nodal joints of geodesic domes made of wood and fiberglass. Publication Series of LAB University of Applied Sciences, part 2. Retrieved on 10 July 2020. Available at https://www.theseus.fi/bitstream/handle/10024/335985/LAB_2020_02.pdf

Online Document (with Author)

Format: Surname, I. & Surname, I. Year. Title. Publisher. Retrieved on [date]. Available at [URL]

Retrieval date is required for all digital sources. No full stop after the URL. If access is restricted, write "Limited availability at".
